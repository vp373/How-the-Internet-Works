IP Addresses

An IP Address is a numerical identifier that is assigned to all devices that are connected to a network. IP stands for internet protocol- you can think of the IP address as the address of your device. IPv4 is a 32 bit number, however, due to the rapid expansion of the internet there are limited addresses left. For this reason, we have been transitioning to IPv6 which is a 128 bit number and allows for many more addresses.

IP Addresses are crucial to how the internet functions. When servers look for addresses to send data packets to, the IP address is what distinguishes your device and ensures delivery to the right place.

Packets

When data is being transferred, it has to be broken down into smaller segments which ae dubbed as packets. Once these packets reach their destination they are reassembled back into the original chunk of data..

Packets are a fundamental part of networks and data transmission. There are 2 things that determine the structure of a packet of data; The protocol and the type of packet.

A good example of the logic behind packets is with large deliveries. For instance, in order to transport a giant airplane before it can fly, you must break it down into a couple different sections, load those sections on trucks, and then reassemble the body of the airplane when it gets to its final destination. Routing

Routing is the process behind how data is sent across networks. There are routing tables which determine the path for these data packets passing through and pass them onto their next location. There image to the right shows that are 3 main types of routing; static routing, dynamic routing,and default routing.

Routing is a fundamental part of data transmission which is the basis behind the internet. Once data packets are split up and sent out to their destination they bounce around intermediate nodes which direct them to their final destination.

An easy example of how routing works is by imagining our mail system. A letter gets sent from one address and has the final destination on the letter. That letter however does not make it straight to the final destination often times. It will be routed a post office where they will then route it to the next post office that brings it closer to its final destination. They repeat this process until the letter ultimately ends up at the final address listed on the letter.
